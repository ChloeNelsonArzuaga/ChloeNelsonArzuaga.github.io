Preparation Table

The kitchen has two preparation tables that slide over the sink and stove. One such table is shown in the animations above. The table has two components. First is the drawer that slides the table off the kitchen. The drawer is powered by a linear slide and supported by two telescoping slides on either side of it.

The second part of the preparation table is a rotating aspect. This part allows for use of the stove/ sink even if there is no space to the side for the drawer to slide out. The rotating part of the table then fits into the existing wall of the kitchen.

The rotation piece is attached to the drawer through a rod that goes through two mounted bearings on either side. The rotating piece can either be screwed or welded to the rod.

The rotating piece is then powered by a motor mounted in the drawer. It is meant to be chain driven between the two gears shown.

Convertible Table

The idea behind the preparation table was that it could replace the many tables people have in their homes for different purposes. The table ranges in height from 18in to 36in. The intended purposes for this table are as follows:

At 18in, it is the height of a coffee table. This makes the table easy to store away.

At 22in, it is the height of a child's play table. This is ideal for families with children because it is often difficult to have a dedicated space for kids to play.

At 27in, it is the height of a wheelchair accessible desk. This means as people age the table can change to accommodate their needs.

At 30in it is the height of a dining room table

At 36in it is the height of a kitchen island/countertop

A portion of the table can then be raised to create a desk from the coffee table high and a bar from the island/countertop.

The design for the scissor lift is a 8bar system such as the one below.

The advantage of this system is that the mounting points on the top and bottom remain stationary. The system is powered by a lead screw that brings the center section closer together. This setup would both allow the table to transform into a desk and bar as described above as well as flatten out into a larger table as shown below.

Design Requirements for Overall Designs:

Modules/Features

Drawer Component

Bases

The base of the drawer is simply composed of a folding panel, a vertical panel (drawer wall), a horizontal sliding panel, and the flex preventers.

Flex Preventers

These small vertical strips of wood attached to the base of the drawer are there to both help prevent the bending caused by putting a load on the drawer, as well as a place to attach the linear bearing to enable sliding.

Sliding Mechanism

Springs

The spring loaded mechanism will assist in part of the actuation for the sliding motion. When the drawer is in it’s closed position, the groove in the spring attached to the horizontal base of the spring mechanism will keep the drawer latched in the closed position, and when a downwards force is applied to that spring, the springs attached to the vertical surface will push against the drawer to their natural displacement, causing the drawer to be actuated away from a closed position.

This is modeled with typical steel spring strips, but can be easily altered with other types of springs like compression springs or rubber springs. These are options that would need to be explored in person.

Concept video for reference: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4GHjI6W6s6M

Linear Actuator

The rest of the sliding motion of the drawer component will be done by a linear actuator. The servocity actuator has a 10” stroke, and a 180 pound thrust. So, it can push up to 180 lbs (which is more than the weight limit of the sliding drawer, so it’s sufficient).

https://www.servocity.com/hda10-30

Linear Bearings

The base drawer will be attached to the frame via an 8020 linear bearing (designed to be coupled with 8020 extrusion pieces), which allows it to slide along the frame with one degree of freedom.

              

https://b2b.partcommunity.com/3d-cad-models/6715-6815-6415-6515-6416-6516-6716-6816-6715-black-6815-black-6415-black-6515-black-6416-black-6516-black-6716-black-6816-black-single-flange-linear-bearings-80-20-inc?info=8020%2Finch%2Flinear_motion%2F6715_asmtab.prj&cwid=9341

Magnets

The springs mechanically actuate the drawer, and the linear actuators slide the drawer to its full extension; to transition from the springs motion to being attached to the linear actuator, magnets are used to snap the rear of the drawer to the shaft of the linear actuator.

This is modeled with discs, but the right strength of magnets are options that would need to be explored in person.

https://www.supermagnete.de/eng/

https://www.supermagnete.de/eng/disc-magnets-neodymium/disc-magnet-30mm-15mm_S-30-15-N

Folding Mechanism

Linear Actuator

The mini linear actuators will help in actuating the folding motion of the drawer, by applying a vertical force to the horizontal countertop panel resting in its flat position. The longest stroke length is 5.51” (140mm), and its closed hole to hole length is 8.19”, which is shorter than the 10” height of the drawer. Depending on the gear ratio (35:1, 63:1,120:1), the maximum thrust ranges from 50N (5kg) to 200N (20kg).

They can be attached to the drawer by pins (as modeled) or screwed through the holes to the inside of the drawer.

Hinges

https://www.mcmaster.com/1460A3

The hinges are used to assist in the folding mechanism. These friction hinges can withstand the torque of the folding panel and allow the panel to open and close in a slow and controlled way. With this, the folding component will be able to remain open at any desired position, and prevent any sudden motions, to help ensure safety.

(Among other friction hinges, these were the thinnest and cleanest looking (for better aesthetic).

Technical Calculations

In order to design the panels to fulfill the design requirements, some simple analysis had to be done to make design choices. The following calculations show the limitations and design choices.

How much flex can the frame withstand?

 

Plywood’s Yield Strength ~13.8 MPa

How much force does it take to push a panel from horizontal to vertical?

The upward force applied against the folding panel by the linear actuator must exceed the downwards force the weight of the panel exerts.

The weight of the panel is ~8N, so the actuator must exert at least 8N. The linear actuator at all three gear ratios supply 50N+ of force, so any choice of gear ratio is okay.

How much torque does it take to rotate a panel from vertical to horizontal and vice versa?

The torque it takes (in pounds*inches) to lift the folding panel tells us the torque rating we need for the friction hinges.

We take the mass of the panel (estimated by Solidworks to me 1.7 pounds) and multiply it by the distance from the location of the hinge to the center of the panel (12.75”), and this results in the total torque

T = 27.675 lb*in.

We divide the total required torque by the number of hinges we want to use, and the torque rating of the hinges must exceed this torque.

T/2 = 10.835 lb*in.

Out of the options on McMaster Carr, I chose the 15lb-in rated hinges.

Fabrication Details

The base of the drawer and the flex panels are made of ⅛’’ plywood; as opposed to 1/16’’, because the thickness will help prevent the drawers from flexing/deforming when load is added.

The base of the spring mechanism is made of ¼” plywood.

The frame of the kitchen used to support the system is made of 8020 extrusions.

All other materials in the assembly can be purchased online and used in the assembly.
