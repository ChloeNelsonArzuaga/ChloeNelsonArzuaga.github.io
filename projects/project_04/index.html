3D Printing Chainmail

This week we were tasked with 3D printing something that could not be made by using a subtractive method. I make chainmail by manually connecting each individual link and was curious if I could 3D print the links already together.

I began this process by creating one ring. The dimensions of this ring were .04 diameter and a 5/16 ID. I chose these dimensions becasue they are the dimensions of the rings I currently have.

4-1 Chainmail is a repeating pattern of 5 links with 1 center ring and 4 rings around it. There are many different types of patterns used for chainmail but I chose this one becasue I am the most familiar with the pattern. Becasue of this I made an assembly in SolidWorks with the 5 rings angled at 45 degrees to try and make the printing process easier.

I then made an assembly with two of the sets of 5 rings and connected then vertically with another ring.

I then connected two of them side by side with 3 rings to get what I actually wanted to print. Going forward I could create as many iterations of this pattern but I wanted my print to be done relativly quickly so I stuck to 4 of my sets of 5

Finally it was time to print! I knew that my rings were vary thin and on the egde of what would print but I wanted to try it anyway. The Rings printed but they were so thin that the printer head was constantly leaving strands and the supports were adheared stronger than the rest of the ring so taking off the supports was all but impossible.

I then went back to by CAD files and increased the thickness of my rings to a little more than half the original thickness. The rings were parametrically defined so all I havd to do was change one dimension and the assemblies updated as well. I then printed this new file and ran into an isue that 2 of the rings were merged because I had not left enough space between the rings to account for the inaccuracy of the printer.
