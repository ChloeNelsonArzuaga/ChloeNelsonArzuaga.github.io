Creating a Programer

This week there was not as much creativity in our designs. We were given the outline of what we were suposed to make. I still really enjoyed this week because I do not know very much (anything) about creating PCB's and was excited to make my own and solder it.

This was what my PCB looked like when it came out of the machine and after I had vacumed away the extra dust that was caused by the cutting. As you can see on the right most edge, my PCB is missing part of the design. This is because when I zeroed the x Axis I wanted to use as little wast as possible so I tried to guestimate how far it should be but was off by a small amount. Luckly the part of the PCB that I am missing does not have any wires so it is not that significant and I did not have to recut the PCB (thankfully becasue it actually took a lot longer than I expected it to becasue it had so many passes that it had to make).

Finally I got around to actually soldering the components to the board. The image above is what my board looked like when it was cleaned up (the extra small peices of copper were removed) and the bottom image is what my final programmer looked like after all of the components were soldered on. It was a challenge to solder all of the peices onto the board because of how small they all were. While we had magnifying glasses, I would continously bump my hand against them and have to wait a while for them to stop shacking so that I could see my peices again. Also for some reason, I could only clearly see the peices thru the magnifying glass with one eye closed (otherwise I would see double and it would make my eyes hurt)
