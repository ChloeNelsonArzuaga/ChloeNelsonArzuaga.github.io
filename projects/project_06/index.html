Creating a Foldable structure

This week we were tasked with making something big. I decided to make a foldale structure that when exapanded was the size of a twin sized bed and when folded was the size of a suitcase. I started by cading everything in SolidWorks.

From my cad drawings I laser cut a quater size model so that I could work out some of the design kinks before moving to the OSB. I found that there was a crack that I had no though aout in the folded out design and tried to minimize it in the future designs.

Finally I got to actually cutting my design out of OSB. The cutting went smoothly except for one pocket that did not get cut. Cleaning the OSB took a vary long time (like 6 hours alone to sand) that I was not expecting. Once all of the wood was clean it also took a while to screw in all the hinges and make all of the joints press together nicely.

I then made a cart so that the structure would e easier to move around that was the correct size. It still took almost an hour to get it home from the N51 shop. I also made a tale that acted as stailization for the top of the structure when it was folded.

Going forward I want to add faric and tent poles to the top of the folded out structure so it actually encloses you. as well as a soft carpert that I have for the floor so it is comfortale.
