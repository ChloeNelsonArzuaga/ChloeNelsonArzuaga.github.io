WaterMaster

Final Project for Toy Product Design

Accelerometer triggered water gun

WaterMaster is an accelerometer triggerd water gun connected to a packpack to store water. By storing water in a backpack, kids could spend more time playing and less having to refil their guns. This also allowed for a more natural movement as the kids simply had to punch and the super light arm bannd would detect their movement and shoot out water.

About me

Consumer products should be funcional and elegent. Since I entered the world of 3-dimensional design, I have strived to create this balance in my research and coursework. I am currenly double majoring in Mechaincal Engineering and Art and Design at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. My path has exposed me to multiple types of design and proceses that allow me the flexability to reach beyond the scope of tradional design. I have learned how to create a design in Solidworks or Rhino, prototype with a 3D printer and laser cutter and create a final product with heavier machinery such as a mille, lathe or injection molding machine. These skills have lead me to become a published researcher and sucsesful student as well as aided me in my endevor to merge mechanical and astetic design.

Beyond research and courses, I have also learned how to create ancient demascus steel and learned how to forge from a japanese master smith. I am always interested in learning new ways to create as well as sharing my knowlage with others. Whether it is in a group project creating toys, or teaching STEM enrichment to faculty and students in Boston and Miami, I belive the sharing of knowledge lage is a worthwhile endevor.
